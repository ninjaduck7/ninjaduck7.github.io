追求完美的代价：当努力超越了合理界限

The Cost of Perfectionism: When Hard Work Exceeds Rational Limits

在科学研究中，精确性和严谨性是基本准则，实验需要控制变量，计算需要精确到小数点后若干位。然而，当这种对精准的追求扩展到日常生活，尤其是个人心理层面时，情况可能就没那么理想了。完美主义与过度反思（即反刍式思维）本质上是对不确定性的过度应对策略。当这种倾向被推至极端，它不仅不会带来更好的结果，反而可能严重损害心理健康，甚至影响实际效率。换句话说，试图最优化一切的过程，本身可能是次优的。

In scientific research, precision and rigor are fundamental principles—experiments require controlled variables, and calculations demand accuracy down to multiple decimal places. However, when this pursuit of precision extends into daily life, particularly in the realm of personal psychology, the results are often suboptimal. Perfectionism and excessive rumination (the tendency to overthink past events) are essentially maladaptive strategies for dealing with uncertainty. When pushed to extremes, these traits not only fail to improve outcomes but can severely impact mental health and even reduce overall efficiency. In other words, the process of optimizing everything may itself be suboptimal.

完美主义在日常生活中的心理代价

The Psychological Cost of Perfectionism in Everyday Life

越来越多的研究表明，完美主义与心理压力之间存在显著的正相关关系。例如，《哈佛商业评论》的一篇文章指出，全球年轻群体中完美主义倾向正在迅速上升，而这种趋势与焦虑、抑郁和慢性压力的增加密切相关。从心理学的角度来看，完美主义者往往对自身要求极高，习惯性地设定难以实现的标准，并在未能达到时陷入自我批评的负反馈循环。更进一步，这种自我施加的压力通常并不会提高实际表现，反而会因过度焦虑而影响决策效率，甚至导致拖延。

A growing body of research highlights a strong positive correlation between perfectionism and psychological stress. For instance, an article in the Harvard Business Review noted that perfectionist tendencies are rising rapidly among young people worldwide, and this trend is strongly linked to increased anxiety, depression, and chronic stress. From a psychological perspective, perfectionists tend to set exceptionally high standards for themselves, engage in relentless self-criticism when they fall short, and become trapped in a negative feedback loop. Paradoxically, this self-imposed pressure often does not enhance actual performance. Instead, excessive anxiety can impair decision-making efficiency and even lead to procrastination.

1. 过度回想错误与尴尬时刻

Overanalyzing Past Mistakes and Embarrassing Moments

  • 在社交场合说错了一句话，几年后仍然会突然想起，并进行无意义的心理模拟：“如果当时换种说法，结果会不会不同？”
  • 在一次演讲或答辩中出现了小失误，尽管没有影响整体结果，但仍然反复回放那个瞬间，并推测观众可能对自己的评价下降。
  • Remembering a social slip-up from years ago and running unnecessary mental simulations: “Would things have gone differently if I had phrased it another way?”
  • Making a minor mistake during a presentation or defense—one that had no real impact on the outcome—but still replaying that moment and speculating on how it altered people’s perceptions.

2. 对职业选择的反思与懊悔

Ruminating Over Career Decisions

  • 在两个接近的工作机会中做出选择后，开始进行事后分析（post hoc analysis），并持续怀疑自己是否选错了。
  • 入职后发现公司文化与预期不符，进而追溯自己的决策过程，并试图找出当初“忽略的变量”，尽管这种反思并不会改变现状。
  • After choosing between two nearly equivalent job offers, engaging in post hoc analysis and persistently questioning whether the other option was better.
  • Realizing after starting a job that the company culture is not as expected, then retrospectively dissecting the decision-making process to identify “overlooked variables”—despite the fact that such reflection does not alter the current situation.

3. 对信息不充分的过度自责

Blaming Oneself for Insufficient Information

  • 在做出重大决策（如搬迁、学术方向选择）后，回顾自己的信息收集过程，并认为当时应该收集更多数据，尽管在当时的约束条件下，这种额外的信息可能并不可获得。
  • 在求职面试后，反思自己是否应该问更多问题，以获取更完整的工作环境信息，即使这些信息在当时可能无法提前获知。
  • After making a major decision (such as relocating or choosing a research direction), retrospectively evaluating the information-gathering process and believing more data should have been collected—even if such data was not realistically accessible at the time.
  • Reflecting post-interview on whether more questions should have been asked to better understand the work environment, despite the fact that certain aspects of a job can only be learned through experience.

4. 对人际互动的过度分析

Overanalyzing Social Interactions

  • 反复回忆某次对话，试图评估自己是否应该使用更精准的表达方式，以避免可能的误解，即使对方可能根本没有注意到这个细节。
  • 朋友回复的语气略显简短，便开始推测对方是否生气，甚至通过过去的交流记录进行相关性分析（尽管统计意义上这往往是无效的）。
  • Replaying a past conversation to assess whether a more precise phrasing should have been used to avoid potential misunderstandings, even though the other person likely did not notice the detail.
  • Noticing a friend’s slightly terse reply and immediately speculating whether they are upset—potentially even conducting an informal correlation analysis with past interactions (which, statistically speaking, is likely meaningless).

5. 害怕犯错导致行动迟滞

Fear of Mistakes Leading to Decision Paralysis

  • 由于希望达到“最优”方案，不断进行额外的计算和权衡，导致实际决策推迟，即“分析瘫痪”（analysis paralysis）。
  • 在撰写邮件或论文时，为了优化措辞，进行过度编辑，导致整体效率下降。
  • Delaying decision-making due to excessive calculations and trade-offs in pursuit of the “optimal” solution, leading to analysis paralysis.
  • Overediting emails or papers in an attempt to perfect phrasing, ultimately reducing overall efficiency.

反刍思维：悲观主义的恶性循环

Rumination: A Vicious Cycle of Negativity

过度的反刍式思维是与完美主义相关的另一种有害现象。从心理学角度来说，“反刍”指持续不断地沉思自己的问题、错误或不足。陷入反刍的人不会着手寻找解决办法或向前迈进，而是停滞在消极思维的循环中。对自身失败的长期关注会加剧心理痛苦，并引发强烈的羞耻、内疚甚至绝望感。在完美主义者身上，这种反刍思维会放大任何不完美所带来的情绪代价，进一步导致抑郁和焦虑等心理问题。

Excessive rumination is another destructive aspect linked to perfectionism. Psychologically, rumination refers to the habit of continuously dwelling on one’s problems, mistakes, or shortcomings. Instead of finding solutions or moving forward, a person who ruminates remains stuck in a cycle of negative thinking. This prolonged focus on perceived failures intensifies distress and can lead to strong feelings of shame, guilt, and even hopelessness. In perfectionists, such rumination magnifies the emotional toll of any imperfection, further contributing to symptoms of depression and anxiety.

如何对抗？从贪心算法的角度正确认知自己的行为

“贪心”不仅仅是算法的策略，也可能是我们思维的陷阱。”

现代心理学对抗完美主义和反刍思维的建议五花八门，诸如运动、心理咨询等等，听起来都挺有道理，但对于内心的 self-doubt（自我怀疑） 和 self-critic（自我批判），这些方法往往只能缓解症状，而不能真正“debug”认知模式。
换个角度看，我们可以把 完美主义 和 反刍思维 视作两种优化策略的不同形态：贪心算法（greedy algorithm） 和 非遍历搜索（non-exploratory search）。

How to Fight It? Correctly Understanding Your Behavior from the Perspective of the Greedy Algorithm

“Greedy” is not just a strategy in algorithms, it can also be a trap in our thinking.”

Modern psychology offers a variety of suggestions for combating perfectionism and rumination, such as exercise, therapy, etc. While they all sound reasonable, when it comes to self-doubt and self-criticism, these methods often only alleviate the symptoms rather than truly “debug” the cognitive patterns.
Looking at it from a different angle, we can view perfectionism and rumination as different forms of optimization strategies: greedy algorithms and non-exploratory search.

The Greedy Algorithm View | 贪心算法视角

A greedy algorithm makes locally optimal choices at each step, hoping to find a global optimum efficiently. However, in complex landscapes, greedy algorithms can easily get stuck in local minima.

贪心算法在每一步都做出局部最优的选择，希望能高效地找到全局最优解。然而，在复杂的能量景观中，贪心算法很容易陷入局部极小值。

Mathematically, we can describe a decision-making process as:

$$
S_{n+1} = \arg\min_{S \in \mathcal{N}(S_n)} f(S)
$$

where $S_n$ is the current state, $\mathcal{N}(S_n)$ is the set of neighboring states, and $f(S)$ represents the cost function (or anxiety function, in our case).

数学上，我们可以将决策过程描述为：

$$
S_{n+1} = \arg\min_{S \in \mathcal{N}(S_n)} f(S)
$$

其中，$S_n$ 是当前状态，$\mathcal{N}(S_n)$ 是其邻域集合，而 $f(S)$ 代表代价函数（在我们的语境中，可以理解为焦虑函数）。

Perfectionism as an Infinite Greedy Loop | 完美主义：无穷贪心循环

Perfectionists attempt to minimize errors at every step, enforcing a greedy approach to optimization. However, if the energy landscape is rugged, such a method can trap them in local minima, preventing progress.

完美主义者试图在每一步都最小化错误，强行执行贪心优化。然而，如果能量景观足够复杂，这种方法会使他们困在局部极小值，无法前进。

One could model perfectionism as an iterative process:

$$
E_{n+1} = \min(E_n, g(E_n))
$$

where $E_n$ represents the current error estimation, and $g(E)$ is an excessively strict correction function. If $g(E)$ is too aggressive, the system never converges smoothly.

我们可以将完美主义建模为一个迭代过程：

$$
E_{n+1} = \min(E_n, g(E_n))
$$

其中 $E_n$ 代表当前的错误估计，$g(E)$ 是一个过于严格的修正函数。如果 $g(E)$ 过于激进，系统就无法平稳收敛。

Rumination as a Non-Ergodic Walk | 反刍思维：非遍历随机行走

Rumination resembles a biased random walk in a high-dimensional space where the walker continuously revisits the same local region without exploring new possibilities.

反刍思维类似于高维空间中的偏置随机行走，行走者不断回到同一区域，而不探索新的可能性。

Formally, this can be modeled as:

$$
S_{n+1} = S_n + \Delta S, \quad \text{where} \quad P(\Delta S) \propto e^{-\beta |\Delta S|}
$$

where $\beta$ controls the rigidity of thought. If $\beta$ is too high, the step size $\Delta S$ becomes small, leading to a cognitive trap.

形式化地，我们可以用以下方程建模：

$$
S_{n+1} = S_n + \Delta S, \quad \text{其中} \quad P(\Delta S) \propto e^{-\beta |\Delta S|}
$$

其中 $\beta$ 控制思维的刚性。如果 $\beta$ 过高，步长 $\Delta S$ 变得极小，导致认知陷阱。

结语

笔者希望从数学贪心算法的角度，将完美主义与反刍思维理解为贪心和非遍历优化问题，为克服这些认知陷阱提供了一种新视角。

I hope to reduce perfectionism and rumination as greedy and non-ergodic optimization problems from the perspective of mathematical greedy algorithms, providing a new viewpoint for overcoming these cognitive traps.
